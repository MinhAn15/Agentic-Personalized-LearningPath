The "One-Size-Fits-All" Crisis

Visual: Comparison table (Chatbots vs. APLO).

Key Points:

  • Statelessness: ChatGPT/Claude treat every session as new; vague memory of user's deep history.
  • Hallucination: LLMs optimize for plausibility, not pedagogical accuracy.
  • Lack of Structure: They don't "see" the dependency map (e.g., Recursion â†’ Binary Search).
  • Result: "Conversational" but not "Structural" learning.

## Section 2: Technical Architecture (Slides 6-15)

Slide 5 / 30

High-Level Architecture

Visual: The 4-Layer Diagram (Frontend, Backend, Agent Layer, Data Layer).

Layers:

  • Interface: Next.js (React) - Interactive Chat & Dashboard.
  • API Gateway: FastAPI - Async, WebSocket support.
  • Orchestration: LlamaIndex Workflows - Event-driven agent coordination.
  • Data Core: Neo4j (Graph) + Milvus (Vector) + Postgres (Relational).
Slide 6 / 30

Innovation 1 - Dual Knowledge Graphs

Visual: Side-by-side nodes.

Course KG (Static):

  • Concepts (Nodes) + Prerequisites (Edges).
  • Source: Ingested from textbooks via Agent 1.

Learner KG (Dynamic):

  • User History + Mastery States.
  • Personalized connections (e.g., "Learner A struggles with Recursion").

Mechanism: Bidirectional Querying (Planner checks both to decide path).

Slide 7 / 30

Innovation 2 - Multi-Agent System (MAS)

Visual: Hexagon diagram of 6 Agents.

The Team:

  • Curator: Builds the world (Course KG).
  • Profiler: Understands the user (Pre-test).
  • Planner: The Architect (Decides what to teach).
  • Tutor: The Teacher (Decides how to teach).
  • Evaluator: The Judge (Updates mastery).
  • Monitor: The Safety Net (Detects stuck users).
Slide 8 / 30

Agent 3 - The Planner (Tree of Thoughts)

Visual: Flowchart of Decision Process.

Algorithm:

  • Scan: Identify all "unlockable" concepts.
  • Filter: Remove mastered concepts.
  • Rank: Score based on Pedagogical Value (Gap filling > New territory).
  • Select: ToT simulation to predict success probability.

Result: Optimal next step, not just random suggestion.

Slide 9 / 30

Agent 4 - The Tutor (Harvard 7 Principles)

Visual: Prompt Template snapshot.

Pedagogy:

  • Not just "Generating text".
  • Principle 1: Connect to prior knowledge (Query Learner KG).
  • Principle 4: Stretch Challenge (Adjust difficulty +0.2 above mastery).
  • Principle 6: Horizontal Connections (Relate to other concepts).
Slide 10 / 30

Agent 5 - The Evaluator (JudgeLM)

Visual: Comparison of Student Answer vs. Reference.

Process:

  • Rubric-based Grading: Correctness (0.6), Completeness (0.2), Clarity (0.2).
  • Feedback Loop: Score updates `MasteryNode` in Neo4j.
  • Threshold: Mastery >= 0.7 triggers "Concept Unlocked".
Slide 11 / 30

GraphRAG Retrieval Pipeline

Visual: Pipeline diagram (Query -> Graph Search + Vector Search -> Context -> LLM).

Why GraphRAG?:

  • Vector alone misses structure (Relationships).
  • Graph alone misses semantics (Nuance).
  • Hybrid: Retrieves Prerequisite Chains (Graph) + Content Chunks (Vector).

Technology Stack

## Section 3: Evaluation & Results (Slides 16-25)

Slide 15 / 30

Evaluation Methodology

Visual: Study Design Flowchart.

  • Type: Simulation-based Ablation Study.
  • Subjects: N=125 Simulated Learners (LLM Agents with distinct profiles).
  • Conditions:
  • Full APLO
  • No Dual-KG
  • No Harvard-7
  • No Multi-Agent
  • Baseline RAG
Slide 16 / 30

Primary Outcome - Learning Gains

Visual: Figure 2 (Box Plot).

Key Finding:

  • APLO: Mean Gain = 84.5%
  • Baseline: Mean Gain = 28.4%
  • Effect Size: Cohen's d = 3.144 (Very Large).

Interpretation: Structural guidance dramatically improves consistency.

Slide 17 / 30

Secondary Outcome - Time to Mastery

Visual: Figure 3 (Bar Chart).

Key Finding:

  • APLO learners reach 70% mastery in 7.6 sessions.
  • Baseline learners take 12.1 sessions.
  • Efficiency: 37% faster learning curve due to targeted gap-filling.
Slide 18 / 30

Why it Works? (Factor Analysis)

Visual: Pie chart or List.

Contributors to Success:

  • Agent Specialization (28% contribution): Modules don't conflict.
  • Dual-KG (20% contribution): Prerequisite enforcement prevents "flailing".
  • Harvard 7 (14% contribution): Better quality explanations.
Slide 19 / 30

Ablation Study Insights

Visual: Table of Delta-d values.

Insight: Removing "Dual-KG" causes the biggest drop in efficiency.

Conclusion: "Knowing what the learner knows" is more important than "Smart explanations".

Slide 20 / 30

Results Contextualization

Visual: Table 4 (Comparison with Real-world).

  • Simulated d: 3.144 (Optimistic).
  • Projected Real-world d: 0.8 - 1.2.
  • Benchmark: Human Tutoring (0.8), Traditional Adaptive (0.35).
  • Takeaway: Even with degradation, APLO outperforms existing systems.
Slide 21 / 30

Simulation vs. Reality Gap

Visual: Scales/Balance graphic.

Factors:

  • Motivation: Simulated learners don't get bored or distracted.
  • Compliance: Simulated learners strictly follow the path.
  • Consistency: AI Agents engage perfectly every time.

Mitigation: Phase 3 Pilot will calibrate this gap.
